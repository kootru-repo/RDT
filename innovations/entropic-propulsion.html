01 Executive Summary

Entropic Propulsion Systems (EPS) represent a theoretical application of thermodynamic gravity (Jacobson 1995, Verlinde 2011) to spacecraft propulsion within the RDT framework. The core premise: if gravity is fundamentally an entropic force emerging from information-theoretic principles, then engineered entropy gradients can produce directional thrust without expelling propellant.

The Paradigm Shift

Classical propulsion: Accelerate by pushing reaction mass backward (F = dp/dt)

Entropic propulsion: Accelerate by tilting spacetime geometry via local entropy gradients

In RDT, you don't push the craft — you curve spacetime so that "at rest" means "moving in the desired direction." This makes acceleration mass-independent, like gravitational freefall.

02 Theoretical Foundation

2.1 Thermodynamic Gravity: The Jacobson-Verlinde Framework

Jacobson (1995): Gravity from Thermodynamics

Ted Jacobson demonstrated that Einstein's field equations emerge from applying the Clausius relation δQ = TδS to local Rindler horizons. This revolutionary result suggests spacetime geometry is not fundamental but emergent from underlying thermodynamic principles.

$$R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}g_{\mu\nu}R + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}T_{\mu\nu}$$
Einstein: spacetime curvature = energy content

Reference: Jacobson, T. "Thermodynamics of Spacetime: The Einstein Equation of State." Phys. Rev. Lett. 75, 1260 (1995).

Verlinde (2011): Gravity as Entropic Force

Erik Verlinde derived Newton's gravitational law from entropy gradients on holographic screens:

$$F = T \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} = T \nabla S$$
Entropy gradient creates emergent force

Gravity is not a fundamental force but an emergent phenomenon — like osmotic pressure or polymer elasticity — arising from the statistical tendency of systems to maximize entropy.

Reference: Verlinde, E. "On the Origin of Gravity and the Laws of Newton." JHEP 1104:029 (2011).

2.3 The Drainage Current

In RDT, gravity is "drainage" — entropy flow between the dual manifolds M+ and M−:

$$J^\mu = \kappa \left( \rho^+ u^\mu_+ - \rho^- u^\mu_- \right)$$
Entropy flows between dual manifolds

Where:

Mass creates drainage vortices — regions where entropy flows preferentially. This IS what we call "gravitational attraction." EPS works by creating artificial drainage asymmetries without mass.

03 Acceleration Models: Force vs. Geometry

There are two distinct models for how entropic thrust might work. RDT predicts Model B, which has profound implications for mass-independence.

Model A: Force-Based

Mechanism: Entropy gradient exerts force on craft

$$F = \kappa T \nabla S$$ $$a = F/M$$

Characteristics:

  • Acceleration inversely proportional to mass
  • Heavy craft = slower acceleration
  • Like electromagnetic force

Problem: 1 GW produces only ~0.3 m/s² for 1000 kg craft

Model B: Geometry-Based

Mechanism: Entropy gradient curves spacetime

$$\nabla S \rightarrow \delta g_{\mu\nu}$$ $$a = \text{geodesic curvature}$$

Characteristics:

  • Acceleration independent of mass
  • All objects follow same geodesic
  • Like gravitational freefall

Advantage: City-sized worldship accelerates same as probe

Why RDT Predicts Model B

In RDT, gravity IS geometry — not a force acting on geometry. The drainage current Jμ doesn't push matter; it modifies the metric gμν itself. Objects then follow geodesics in this modified metric.

This is exactly how gravity works in General Relativity: a feather and a bowling ball fall at the same rate because they're both following geodesics, not being "pushed" by a force proportional to their mass.

EPS doesn't push the craft. EPS tilts spacetime so "at rest" means "moving."
