2. RDT Foundations for Dark Energy

F4: CPT Symmetry with Dual Manifolds

RDT's Foundation F4 posits that reality consists of two CPT-conjugate manifolds sharing a common past boundary:

M⁺ (our universe) ←— Σ (common origin) —→ M⁻ (CPT mirror)

Key properties:

  • Σ (common boundary): Minimum entropy state, CPT-symmetric
  • M⁺: Our universe with time flowing "forward" (entropy increasing)
  • M⁻: CPT-conjugate with time flowing "backward" (also entropy increasing from Σ)
  • Conservation: Total information conserved across M⁺ ∪ M⁻

The Entropy Differential

At any cosmic time τ, each manifold has entropy S⁺(τ) and S⁻(τ). The entropy differential between them:

ΔS(τ) ≡ S⁺(τ) - S⁻(τ)

Due to CPT symmetry, both manifolds evolve identically in their respective time directions, so ΔS measures how much our universe has "diverged" from the symmetric initial state.

Key Insight: ΔS Drives Effective Λ

In RDT, the cosmological "constant" Λ isn't fundamental — it's the drainage rate imbalance between M⁺ and M⁻. As the universes evolve and ΔS grows, this drainage imbalance changes, making Λ_eff dynamical.

Component A: Entropy Differential Evolution

From F4, both manifolds start at Σ with S⁺(0) = S⁻(0) = S_min. As cosmic time τ progresses, entropy grows in both, but with potential asymmetry:

A1 Normalized differential:

δ(τ) ≡ ΔS(τ) / S_total = tanh(τ/τ_*)

The tanh form emerges from bounded entropy growth: δ → 0 as τ → 0 (symmetric start), δ → 1 as τ → ∞ (maximum divergence). Here τ_* is the characteristic divergence time, related to the age of the universe.

A2 Redshift parameterization:

δ(z) = δ_0 · [1 - (1+z)/(1+z_*)] for z < z_*

Converting to redshift z (where z = 0 is today, higher z is earlier), the differential was smaller in the past. The transition redshift z_* ≈ 0.5-2 marks when the differential becomes cosmologically significant.

Component D: Drainage Function and Effective Λ

RDT describes gravity as entropy "drainage" toward massive configurations. The drainage function Ψ modifies gravitational effects at cosmological scales:

D1 Drainage rate dependence on ΔS:

Ψ(δ) = 1 - ε · δ

When δ = 0 (symmetric state), Ψ = 1 (standard gravity). As the entropy differential grows, drainage efficiency decreases slightly. The parameter ε ~ O(0.1) sets the coupling strength.

D2 Effective cosmological constant:

Λ_eff(z) = Λ_0 · Ψ(δ(z))² = Λ_0 · [1 - ε·δ(z)]²

The squared dependence comes from the energy density scaling: ρ_Λ ∝ Λ, and Λ emerges from the drainage rate squared (dimensional analysis from drainage current J^μ).

D3 Equation of state from Λ evolution:

w(z) = -1 + (1+z)/3 · d ln Λ_eff/dz

Standard result: for any evolving dark energy density ρ_DE(z), the equation of state w relates to the logarithmic derivative. For pure Λ (constant), this gives w = -1.

d ln Λ_eff/dz ≈ δ_w/(1+z_*) · 1/[1 + ((1+z)/(1+z_*) - 1)·(something)]

Since δ_w > 0 (entropy differential grows with time), n > 0 (quadratic), and z_* > 0, we have w_a < 0. This means:
